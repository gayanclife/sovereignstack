# Community Conduct

This project follows the Contributor Covenant v2.1 community standard:

> https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/

## In short

Be respectful. Disagree on technical decisions, not on people. Assume good
faith. If a conversation is going badly, take a break before responding.

## What we expect

- Use welcoming and inclusive language.
- Respect differing viewpoints and experience levels.
- Accept constructive feedback gracefully.
- Focus on what's best for the project and the community.
- Show empathy toward other community members.

## What's not OK

- Harassment, insults, derogatory comments, or personal attacks.
- Public or private harassment of any kind.
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission.
- Other conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a
  professional setting.

## Scope

These standards apply in all project spaces — issues, PRs, discussions,
chat, and any public communication that represents the project. They also
apply at events where a maintainer is representing SovereignStack.

## Reporting

Report violations to `conduct@sovereignstack.io`. Reports are confidential.
We will respond within 5 business days with an initial assessment and
proposed next steps.

We retain the right to remove comments, commits, code, issues, or other
contributions that violate these standards, and to ban temporarily or
permanently any contributor whose behavior we deem inappropriate,
threatening, offensive, or harmful.

## Enforcement

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying the standards and taking
appropriate corrective action. Maintainers who do not follow or enforce
these standards in good faith may face temporary or permanent removal from
the project's leadership.
